# cadence — the master clock of your business

> The third C of the **Captain's Flywheel** (Command · Compute · Cadence).
> This folder holds the rhythms that make your business compound.

## What lives here

Five files, one per cadence:

- **[daily.md](./daily.md)** — the build cadence. Ship something every day.
- **[weekly.md](./weekly.md)** — content production, captain's call with the team, weekly review.
- **[monthly.md](./monthly.md)** — strategic checkpoint, security review, financial scan.
- **[quarterly.md](./quarterly.md)** — OKRs, kill-list, strategic muscle, comp + role conversations.
- **[yearly.md](./yearly.md)** — the 10-hour retrospective + theme + 3 big bets.

Each file is a working template. You edit it as you run the rhythm. Over time, your version drifts from the seed — that's the point. The folder is yours.

## Why all five

Most operators run one cadence (daily) and call it operations. Daily is necessary and not sufficient. The captains who compound run **all five** in parallel. Skip the slower ones and the work stops compounding.

The full essay is at [max-ship.com/log/cadence/master-cadence](https://max-ship.com/log/cadence/master-cadence) — read it once, then come back here and edit your daily.md.

## How AI uses this folder

When you talk to Claude (or any AI tool with filesystem access) about today's work, point it at `cadence/daily.md`. When it's quarterly review time, point it at `cadence/quarterly.md` plus the previous `cadence/yearly.md`. The folder is the captain's brief. The AI reads what you've written and stays in your context.

Most operators paste the same context into ChatGPT every time. Captains running this folder don't.

## How the cadences stack

```
daily ships  →  weekly review packages them
weekly outputs  →  monthly retrospective
monthly retros (×3)  →  quarterly OKR + kill-list
quarterly retros (×4)  →  yearly retrospective
yearly retro  →  next year's command/strategy/
```

The compound only happens because each cadence feeds the next.

## Adapting to your business

The templates are scaffolding. The opinions are mine. Edit freely:

- Your "ship something every day" might mean a customer call, not a PR.
- Your weekly captain's call might be solo until you have a team.
- Your quarterly kill-list might just be a kill-line until you have a list.

The structure is the contract. The content is yours.
